Zappos & Holacracy

When a company famous for its culture uproots itself, the entire world is held in suspense.

By Ryan O'Shea, Tess Marchant, Jennie Werner, Lawrence Yu, & Annie Tarakchian.
Presented as a final project for SOC 346: Sociology of the Cubicle at Princeton University.

For the past couple of years, Zappos has been in transition: moving from a traditional bureaucratic management system to a revolutionary new setup: a Holacracy. The radical transition, while bumpy for the company, has caught the attention of the media and the public at large; everyone is interested to see how Zappos will implement a Holacracy and whether it will be successful. We took a deep look at this bold move through the lenses of rituals, charismatic authority, reinforcement politics, and isomorphism.
